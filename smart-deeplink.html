What its DeepLink? 

deep links route with your app URI, which is like a web domain for your app - instead of https://mysite.com/ page, you get a link that looks like this: myapp://page . Most apps already have a URI scheme, but if not you can easily register for one. The problem with traditional deep links is that they don't work if you don't have the app installed. The phone will look for the app content at the address given by the URI scheme: if the app isn't installed, the phone can't find the content and will show an error.

Whats Smart DeepLink?

Smart DeepLink look similar to web links, but act differently depending on whether or not an app is installed. If the app is installed, they link straight to the content, like traditional deep links. If the app is not installed, then the link will direct to the relevant app store to download the app, and remember the content that a user was linked to.


When the app has been downloaded, the user can be taken to specific content immediately. The presentation of specific content is "deferred" until after the install, then shown to the user.
